Linux mdraid And RAID Levels

Offline notes derived from official Ubuntu Server and Linux mdraid documentation.

Linux software RAID is commonly managed through mdraid and the mdadm tool. RAID 0 emphasizes throughput without redundancy, RAID 1 mirrors data for protection, RAID 5 and RAID 6 use parity for resilience with capacity tradeoffs, and RAID 10 combines mirrored protection with stronger performance at higher disk cost.

For AI servers, RAID decisions are usually about local dataset caches, scratch space, and checkpoint durability. RAID does not replace a shared storage architecture, but it does influence local server performance, rebuild behavior, and data protection tradeoffs.

Why It Matters For AI Infrastructure

Operators need to understand whether the local storage path is optimized for speed, redundancy, or a balance of both. That decision affects node rebuilds, data safety, and the reliability of local caches used by training or preprocessing jobs.
